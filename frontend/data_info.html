1. 📏 Length / Distance

Length measures the distance between two points. Units include metric (meter, cm, km) and imperial (inch, ft, yd, mi).

UnitMeaning
mMeter — base unit of length in metric system.
cmCentimeter — 1/100 of a meter.
mmMillimeter — 1/1000 of a meter.
kmKilometer — 1000 meters.
inchInch — 2.54 cm.
ftFoot — 12 inches = 0.3048 m.
ydYard — 3 feet = 0.9144 m.
miMile — 1.609 km.

2. ⚖️ Mass / Weight

Mass measures the amount of matter in an object. Weight is mass influenced by gravity. Units include grams, kilograms, pounds, ounces, etc.

UnitMeaning
gGram — base unit of mass.
kgKilogram — 1000 grams.
mgMilligram — 1/1000 of a gram.
lbPound — 0.4536 kilograms.
ozOunce — 1/16 of a pound.
tonTonne — 1000 kilograms (metric ton).

3. ⏱ Time

Time units measure durations from seconds to years. They are widely used in science, daily activities, and technology.

UnitMeaning
sSecond — base unit of time.
minMinute — 60 seconds.
hHour — 60 minutes.
dDay — 24 hours.
wkWeek — 7 days.
yrYear — 365 days (approx).

4. 🌡 Temperature

Temperature measures hotness or coldness. Common units: Celsius, Fahrenheit, Kelvin.

UnitMeaning
°CCelsius — metric scale.
°FFahrenheit — imperial scale.
KKelvin — absolute thermodynamic scale.

5. 🗺 Area

Area measures surface size. Units range from square meters to acres and hectares.

UnitMeaning
m²Square meter — base unit.
cm²Square centimeter — 1/10000 m².
km²Square kilometer — 1,000,000 m².
acre4046.86 m².
hectare10,000 m².

6. 🧴 Volume

Volume measures space occupied by an object or liquid.

UnitMeaning
LLiter — base unit of volume.
mLMilliliter — 1/1000 L.
cm³Cubic centimeter — equal to 1 mL.
m³Cubic meter — 1000 L.
galGallon — 3.785 L (US).

7. 🏎 Speed

Speed measures distance traveled over time. Units include metric and imperial systems, as well as nautical speed.

UnitMeaning
m/sMeters per second.
km/hKilometers per hour.
mphMiles per hour.
knKnot — nautical mile per hour.

8. 🔧 Pressure

Pressure measures force applied over an area. Units include Pascal, bar, atmosphere, and psi.

UnitMeaning
PaPascal — 1 N/m².
bar1 bar = 100,000 Pa.
atmAtmosphere — 101,325 Pa.
psiPound per square inch.

9. ⚡ Energy

Energy is the capacity to do work. Units include Joule, calorie, kilojoule, and kilowatt-hour.

UnitMeaning
JJoule — base SI unit.
kJKilojoule — 1000 Joules.
calCalorie — 4.184 Joules.
kWhKilowatt-hour — 3.6 million Joules.

10. ⚙️ Power

Power is the rate of energy transfer. Units include Watt, kilowatt, and horsepower.

UnitMeaning
WWatt — 1 Joule per second.
kWKilowatt — 1000 watts.
hpHorsepower — 745.7 watts.

11. 🎵 Frequency

Frequency measures cycles per second. Units include Hertz, Kilohertz, Megahertz, Gigahertz.

UnitMeaning
HzHertz — 1 cycle per second.
kHzKilohertz — 1000 Hz.
MHzMegahertz — 1,000,000 Hz.
GHzGigahertz — 1,000,000,000 Hz.

12. 🔄 Angle

Angles measure rotation or inclination. Units include degrees, radians, and gradians.

UnitMeaning
°Degree — 1/360 of a full rotation.
radRadian — 180/π degrees.
gradGradian — 1/400 of a full rotation.

13. 💾 Data Storage

Data storage units measure digital information.

UnitMeaning
bitBinary digit — smallest unit.
BByte — 8 bits.
KBKilobyte — 1024 bytes.
MBMegabyte — 1024 KB.
GBGigabyte — 1024 MB.
TBTerabyte — 1024 GB.

14. 🌐 Digital Speed

Digital speed measures data transfer rate over time.

UnitMeaning
bpsBits per second.
KbpsKilobits per second.
MbpsMegabits per second.
GbpsGigabits per second.

15. 🏋️ Force

Force measures the push or pull on an object.

UnitMeaning
NNewton — force to accelerate 1 kg by 1 m/s².
kNKilonewton — 1000 Newtons.
dyne1 dyne = 10⁻⁵ Newton.
lbfPound-force.

16. 🧪 Density

Density measures mass per unit volume.

UnitMeaning
kg/m³Kilogram per cubic meter.
g/cm³Gram per cubic centimeter.
lb/ft³Pound per cubic foot.

17. 💡 Illuminance

Illuminance measures light intensity on a surface.

UnitMeaning
lxLux — lumen per square meter.
fcFoot-candle — lumen per square foot.

18. ☢️ Radioactivity

Radioactivity measures decay of unstable atoms.

UnitMeaning
BqBecquerel — 1 disintegration per second.
CiCurie — 3.7×10¹⁰ disintegrations per second.

19. 🌌 Astronomy Distance

Measures distances in space, from the Earth-Sun distance to light-years and parsecs.

UnitMeaning
AUAstronomical Unit — distance from Earth to Sun (~149.6 million km).
lyLight-year — distance light travels in one year.
pcParsec — 3.26 light-years.
